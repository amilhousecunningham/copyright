Question # 1 - What is Copyright?

Answer: The U.S. Copyright Office defines copyright as a "type of intellectual property that protects original works of authorship as soon as an author fixes the work in a tangible form of expression" (U.S. Copyright Office). Copyright law protects original and fixed works. Original works are "independently created by a human author and have a minimal degree of creativity" (U.S. Copyright Office). However, fixed works are "captured (either by or under the authority of an author) in a sufficiently permanent medium such that the work can be perceived, reproduced, or communicated for more than a short time" (U.S. Copyright Office). Question # 2 - What is Fair Use?

Answer: The Stanford Copyright and Fair Use Center defines fair use as "any copying of copyrighted material done for a limited and “transformative” purpose, such as to comment upon, criticize, or parody a copyrighted work" (Stim, 2021). In these cases, permission from the copyright owner is not required. Click the links below for more details.
